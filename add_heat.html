Additively Manufactured Heat Sinks for High-Reliability Applications

In high-reliability extreme environment electronics applications such as built-in temperature and vibration sensors for turbine blades, there has been a push to produce higher feature densities capable of more on-board computation. This computation generates heat, which needs to be removed and transferred to a working fluid, such as air or a dedicated cooling fluid.

To both increase the surface area of the pins and improve manufacturability, we designed additively manufactured hybrid DLP and metal electroformed heat sink pins for these environmental applications. The design includes a reliable sensitization and electroforming technique for plating printed pins.
